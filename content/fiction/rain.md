## Part One — The Storm

The rain arrived without warning, the way it does in early autumn — one moment the sky was merely grey, the next the street was a river. She ran for the nearest doorway. He was already there. They stood shoulder to shoulder watching the downpour, close enough that she could feel warmth radiating from his jacket.

## Part Two — Waiting

They started talking because there was nothing else to do. His name was ordinary and she forgot it immediately and then couldn't ask again. He had a dry way of observing things that made her laugh when she wasn't expecting to. The rain showed no interest in stopping. At some point their shoulders stopped being merely adjacent and started leaning, just slightly.

## Part Three — The Clearing

[italic]
When the rain eased he said 'I should probably—' and she said 'Yes, probably.' Neither of them moved. Then he said 'There's a coffee place around the corner if the rain comes back,' which was a very thin pretext and they both knew it. She said she'd heard it was very good. They walked around the corner in the last of the rain.
